What is IELTS?

IELTS stands for the International English Language Testing System. It is a standardized test designed to assess the English language proficiency of non-native English speakers who wish to study or work in English-speaking countries. The test measures four language skills: listening, reading, writing, and speaking.

Type of Exams

IELTS Academic: This version is for individuals who want to study at an undergraduate or postgraduate level or for professional registration purposes in an English-speaking environment. It assesses whether you are ready to begin studying or training in an environment where English is the language used.

IELTS General Training: This version is for individuals who are going to English-speaking countries for secondary education, work experience, or training programs. It is also a requirement for migration to

Australia, Canada, New Zealand, and the UK. This version assesses basic survival skills in broad social and workplace contexts.

IELTS Score?

IELTS scores are typically reported on a scale from 0 to 9 for each section (listening, reading, writing, and speaking), as well as an overall band score, which is the average of the four section scores. Each band score corresponds to a level of English proficiency, ranging from non-user (band score 1) to expert (band score 9). Different institutions and organizations have their own score requirements for admission, employment, or immigration purposes

Score valid

Once you've taken the IELTS test, you can view your results online 13 days after the test date. The Test Report Form (TRF) is then valid for two years, allowing you to use it for various purposes such as university admissions, immigration applications, or employment opportunities during that time frame.
